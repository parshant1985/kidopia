🌟 How to Start Your Own Campaign

Every big idea begins with a small step! Starting your own campaign is your chance to share what you care about and inspire others to join your journey. Here’s how you can begin:

1.Dream It ✨ – Think about a problem you want to solve or a change you’d love to see.

2.Plan It 📋 – Write down your goal and make a simple action plan.

3.Share It 📣 – Tell your friends, family, and community about your campaign.

4.Grow It 🌱 – Be creative! Use posters, events, or social media to spread your idea.

5.Lead It 🏆 – Show confidence, stay motivated, and encourage others to take part.

👉 Remember, every campaign—big or small—starts with you believing in your idea. Take the first step today and watch your dream grow!



💰 How to Fund Your Campaign

Every idea needs a little fuel to get started—and that fuel is funding! Don’t worry, funding doesn’t have to be scary. With creativity and teamwork, you can raise the support you need. Here’s how:

1.Start Small 🌱 – Begin with your savings or pocket money. Even a little can go a long way!

2.Ask for Support 🙌 – Share your campaign with family, friends, and neighbors. They’ll love to help you grow.

3.Offer Something Back 🎁 – Create simple thank-you cards, badges, or shoutouts for your supporters.

4.Be Creative 🎨 – Host a bake sale, art show, or mini-event to raise funds in a fun way.

5.Go Digital 💻 – Use safe online platforms (with parent help!) to share your idea and invite contributions.

👉 Remember, funding isn’t just about money—it’s about building a team of people who believe in your dream.

Stories of Young Entrepreneurs

Success Stories

These real-life journeys showcase how individuals and teams turned challenges into opportunities and achieved remarkable outcomes. From overcoming obstacles to reaching breakthrough milestones, these stories serve as a powerful reminder that with the right mindset and effort, success is always within reach. Let their achievements motivate and energize you for what’s possible.



Inspiring Kidpreneur Success Stories

1. Alina Morse – Zolli Candy At age 10, Alina launched Zolli Candy, creating sugar-free lollipops known as Zollipops, followed by Zolli Drops and Zaffi Taffy. By age 13, her products were sold in ~25,000 stores in the U.S. and globally, with $6M in sales in 2018. She became the youngest person ever featured on the cover of Entrepreneur Magazine and was invited twice to the White House by Michelle Obama.

2. Mikaila Ulmer – Me & the Bees Lemonade Age 4: After being stung by bees, Mikaila began selling lemonade sweetened with local honey to promote bee conservation. Eventually, she appeared on Shark Tank, securing $60,000 for 25% equity, and now her lemonade is sold in over 1,500 stores.

3. Moziah Bridges – Mo’s Bows Age 9: Moziah began crafting handmade bow ties with his grandmother's help and launched Mo’s Bows on Etsy. His brand caught media attention, landed partnerships with Neiman Marcus and Bloomingdale’s, and even secured an NBA license deal.

4. Rachel Zietz – Lacrosse Gear Line Started at 13: Rachel developed a line of durable lacrosse equipment. By the second year of business, she had generated $1 million in revenue..

5. Braxton Harst – Braxton Bakes 13 years old: Overcame bullying through baking; his TikTok baking videos went viral, leading to nationwide cake orders and brand collaborations (e.g., Barbie, Betty Crocker). He now runs “Braxton Bakes,” aiming to turn his passion into a thriving business.

6. Annie Rogers – My Voice Communications & Creative Jewelry Age 16: While still in high school, Annie founded a communications company that developed a necklace device to give a voice to non-verbal individuals—inspired by her friend with cerebral palsy. She also runs a polymer clay earring business, started as a creative outlet during her recovery from injury. She’s been honored as National Young Entrepreneur of the Year.

7. Youth Entrepreneurs from South Australia A feature highlights a range of young entrepreneurs: Noah Pronk (12): Created Sticky Pronk, an eco-friendly surf wax business, now widely stocked and profitable. Other rising stars with businesses including popcorn, candle making, dog accessories, hair styling, frozen treats, and more—all showcasing age is no barrier to enterprise.

Life Skills for Young Leaders

Whether speaking in front of a crowd or writing an email, communication is everything. Leaders listen well, speak clearly, and know how to tailor their message.

Strong leaders don’t just follow — they think. Critical thinking helps young people evaluate information, weigh options, and make smart choices. Practice: Debates, case studies, ethical dilemmas.

Leadership starts from within. Young leaders need to know their values, strengths, and areas for growth. Practice: Journaling, feedback sessions, personality assessments.

STEM & Creativity

When you think of STEM — science, technology, engineering, and math — you might imagine formulas, codes, and lab reports. But here’s the truth:

Problem Solving Needs Innovation Every great invention started with a question and a spark of imagination. Creativity helps students approach problems from unique angles. Tomorrow’s careers will require both coding and creative thinking, engineering and empathy, data and design.

  • Project-Based Learning Let students design their own inventions, apps, or scientific experiments — and present them creatively.
  • Arts Integration (STEAM) Adding the "A" for Arts turns STEM into STEAM, reminding us that design, storytelling, and visual thinking enhance technical work.
  • Makerspaces & Innovation Labs Hands-on environments where students tinker, build, prototype, and create with tech and tools.
